Project Juggler

Our Vertical Slice

The vertical slice for our game Project Juggler is comprised of a complete level with an introductory tutorial where the player entirely controls a mech that can move, look around, and shoot within a three-dimensional environment, controlling similarly to a first-person shooter.

As an additional challenge, however, the player must also maintain their own physical and mental wellbeing; this is done by navigating within the mech to complete tasks such as drinking tea or doing homework.

Players must defend the city's residents from invading robots while keeping themselves in peak condition.

Our Design Constraints

Accessibility

1. Vision

- We ensured to make our visuals distinct with contrast-y and bright outlines, shapes, and colors to make identification and pattern recognition as organic as possible even in the absence of color.


2. Touch

- We've integrated an "auto-pilot" mode that enables the mech to automatically move, aim, and fire towards the nearest enemy, which in turn allows one-handed play through the use of only the mouse.


3. Hearing

- Vision takes precedence over audio, so there are many visual indicators of events that are happening within the game such as a task needing to be complete, enemies spawning on the screen, or when the mech is taking damage. Audio only serves to enhance the experience, so players who cannot hear will be able to perform about as well as those can hear.


4. Rest

- There is a two-minute cooldown between enemy waves for the player to recollect themselves, but in the case that the player is idle for a specific period of time, the game engages auto-pilot mode automatically.


Localization

Our game has a dropdown menu for changing the language of the game between English and Japanese; some developers on the team were fluent or otherwise familiar with the dialect and could thus proofread for mistakes and ensure accurate translation.


Game Format

Offline Game

1. Must be able to run offline

- Our game does not utilize nor connect to the internet during any given play session.


2. Total download size less than 100 MB

- Through compression settings and other optimizations with assets like textures, we managed to trim our zipped game build down to at most 40 MB for all platforms.


3. Must remember player progress across play sessions

- We integrated a settings menu that allows the player to customize options that persist between game sessions and launches through a JSON file.


4. Can use any platform type (only one is required)

- We have downloadable and playable game builds available for both Mac and Windows computers.
